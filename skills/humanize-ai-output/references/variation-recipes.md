# Variation Recipes

Use a recipe to make repeated runs on the same prompt naturally different. Change style, rhythm, structure, and emphasis; never change facts.

## Recipes

| Recipe | Best for | Moves |
|---|---|---|
| Concise practical | Work updates, emails, internal notes | Cut openings, keep concrete next steps, use short paragraphs |
| Conversational professional | Client replies, social posts, founder notes | Keep a human voice, add light connective phrases, avoid stiff polish |
| Editorial | Essays, newsletters, analysis | Open with tension, vary sentence length, use precise nouns |
| Warm explanatory | Teaching, onboarding, customer support | Use plain language, soften commands, add context sparingly |
| Sharp executive | Investor updates, strategy slides | Claim first, evidence second, risk third, action last |
| Loose draft | Early brainstorming, creator content | Leave some texture, avoid over-finishing, preserve voice |
| Visual director | Image prompts and design briefs | Start with purpose, then composition, light, materials, constraints |
| Document editor | Reports and PPT | Turn topics into claims, remove decorative structure, add hierarchy |

## Seed Dimensions

Pick at least three:

- Opening: direct finding, scene detail, problem tension, decision, user pain, concrete next step.
- Rhythm: short lines, mixed paragraph lengths, one expanded paragraph, memo bullets.
- Intervention: light polish, medium rewrite, heavy restructure.
- Detail: spare, grounded specifics from source, action rows, examples.
- Layout: claim slide, decision memo, evidence table, timeline, owner/action grid.

## Do Not

- Randomize numbers, source references, named entities, dates, or obligations.
- Add personal experience unless the user supplied it.
- Make the writing sound intentionally imperfect by adding typos.
- Turn professional content into slang without permission.
