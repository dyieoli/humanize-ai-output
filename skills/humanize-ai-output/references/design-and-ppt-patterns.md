# Design And PPT Patterns

Use this reference for PPT, document, report, landing-page copy, design briefs, and layout critique.

## AI-Ish Layout Symptoms

| Pattern | Symptoms | Better move |
|---|---|---|
| Card spam | Every idea appears inside rounded cards; cards inside cards | Use cards only for repeated items or framed tools |
| Equal-weight everything | All metrics, bullets, icons, and boxes look equally important | Pick one dominant takeaway and subordinate the rest |
| Decorative hierarchy | Huge title, generic subtitle, three shallow boxes | Put the actual finding, chart, object, decision, or screenshot in the first view |
| Generic iconography | Rockets, sparkles, targets, puzzle pieces, lightbulbs | Use domain-specific visuals or no icon |
| Gradient/orb background | Purple-blue gradients, glass panels, floating blobs | Use brand colors, real imagery, neutral surfaces, or data-led composition |
| Fake diagrams | Arrows and circles imply flow but encode no real causality | Build diagrams from real sequence, ownership, dependency, timeline, or funnel |
| Bullet overload | Paragraphs chopped into bullets | Keep one idea per bullet; move detail to notes or appendix |
| Unanchored metrics | Big numbers without baseline, source, period, or implication | Add denominator, date, source, and why it matters |
| Stock symmetry | 3 columns, 4 quadrants, centered title on every page | Let the slide type decide layout: decision, evidence, timeline, comparison, appendix |

## PPT Rewrite Moves

- Turn topic titles into claim titles.
  - Before: "Market Opportunity"
  - After: "The opportunity is real, but adoption is stuck at workflow integration"
- Replace four abstract dimensions with sequence.
  - Before: "organization / process / technology / data"
  - After: "First fix ownership, then standardize handoffs, then automate the repeated checks"
- Replace generic recommendations with action rows.
  - Owner, next step, input needed, deadline, risk, success signal.
- Use tables when the reader needs comparison.
- Use timelines when the reader needs sequencing.
- Use annotated screenshots when the reader needs to see the product or workflow.

## Document De-Templating

For reports and memos:

- Start with the actual tension, decision, or change.
- Cut "market is changing rapidly" unless the specific change is named.
- Keep executive summaries short and answer-driven.
- Use section titles that say what the section concludes.
- Preserve citations and source hierarchy.

## Design Brief Guidance

For visual output:

- Define audience, use case, visual hierarchy, assets, color constraints, and prohibited tropes.
- Make spacing, alignment, typography, and content density explicit.
- Avoid prompting for a "beautiful modern design" without content hierarchy.

## Final Check

- Does the slide or document have one clear job?
- Does layout reflect priority?
- Are visual elements carrying information, or only decoration?
- Would a designer know what to build without guessing?
